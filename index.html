Wood glue

For many centuries animal glue, especially hide glue, was the primary adhesive of choice for a great many types of woodworking, including furniture and lutherie. It is manufactured from rendered collagen from the skins (hides) of animals. It is chemically similar to edible gelatin and is non-toxic if ingested.
